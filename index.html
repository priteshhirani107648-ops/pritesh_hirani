Knowledge_Base

Hover to decrypt Physical Design terminology.

Floorplanning

Core Setup

Defining the size, shape, and macro placement of the chip. Establishes the foundation for power routing and area utilization.

Placement

Standard Cells

Assigning exact physical locations to standard cells to optimize wirelength, timing, and alleviate routing congestion.

CTS

Clock Tree Synthesis

Distributing the clock signal evenly to all sequential elements to minimize clock skew and insertion delay.

Routing

Metal Interconnects

Creating physical metal connections between cells based on the logical netlist while strictly avoiding DRC violations.

IR Drop

Power Integrity

Voltage drop in the power grid due to wire resistance ($V = I \times R$). Excessive drop degrades cell performance and causes timing failures.

Signoff

DRC & LVS

Final physical verification. DRC ensures manufacturability, while LVS ensures the physical layout matches the logical netlist.

ECO

Engineering Change Order

Making late-stage, surgical changes to the netlist to fix functional or timing bugs without doing a full, time-consuming re-spin of the design.

Setup / Hold

Timing Constraints

Setup: Data must be stable before the clock edge.
Hold: Data must remain stable after the clock edge.
